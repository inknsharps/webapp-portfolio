About Me

Neil Chen is a New York City based freelance musician, calligrapher, music typesetter and full stack developer.

Praised for his ability to "deftly underpin the music's harmonic structure," (Nelson Mail, 2017), Neil graduated from The Juilliard School in 2017, completing a Masters of Historical Performance majoring in bassoon. Throughout his experience working with historical instruments, he has always been searching for, and typesetting repertoire, and finally decided it was time to share and make this music more accessible through his own editions and providing resources for others who are interested in the music searching process. As a performer, he has performed with Juilliard415, Les Arts Florissants, Bach Vespers at Holy Trinity, and the Toronto Chamber Choir.

His interest in calligraphic and lettering arts arose from viewing countless calligraphic manuscripts while studying treatises and primary sources of music. He began self teaching himself script from those sources, and ended up focusing on pointed pen calligraphy after being inspired by original work on IAMPETH in 2015. He has studied with Elinor Holland, and has taken workshops by Harvest Crittenden, James "Fazz" Farrell, David Grimes, Loredana Zega.

He is currently attending the Columbia Engineering Bootcamp.
